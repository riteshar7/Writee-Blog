Increase Sales with AI: 5 Best Practices for High Converting Sales Emails

Using AI in sales emails can be an incredibly effective way to increase sales.

Prannay Kedia

•

February 19, 2023

Writing effective sales emails can make the difference between a sale v/s no sale. Whether you're reaching out to potential customers, closing a sale, or requesting feedback, you must craft persuasive emails that capture people's attention. In this blog, we'll explore how to write persuasive emails for sales, develop an email strategy, and utilize best practices to increase engagement and conversions. With the right approach, you can drive more leads and sales while building strong relationships with customers.

How to Write Sales Emails?

Writing effective sales emails requires a few key components:

  1. You need to choose the right words and style of language to capture the attention of potential customers.
  2. You must craft a compelling headline that piques the reader’s interest.
  3. The topic of your email should offer a clear solution to the customer’s problem or need.

To write an effective sales email, start by researching your target audience and coming up with an appropriate title. Use a clear, conversational style of writing to convey your message. Consider the tone of your email – it should be confident but not aggressive. Finally, make sure the email ends with a call to action, such as directing the customer to your website, telephone number or other relevant contact information. With a compelling sales email, your copywriting skills can bring more leads and higher revenue for your business.

Top 5 Best Practices for Writing Engaging Sales Emails

Here are 5 best practices to follow while writing sales emails:

  1. Personalize the message
  2. Focus on benefits, not features
  3. Include Relevant CTAs for High ROI
  4. Keep it short & sweet
  5. Incorporate elements of design

Personalize the message - create and foster relationships

AI has opened up a new way of targeting a wider range of customers. With AI-powered personalised messages, businesses can send unique emails to each customer. These messages are written based on their interests and previous purchases, making them more likely to open and engage with an email. This allows a business to create a better customer experience and keep its consumers hooked.

This also allows copywriters the chance to measure the impact of their emails on the audience, allowing for more informed decisions. Automated email campaigns can also be set up, allowing copywriters to send emails at the right times, sending messages at the optimal times for a customer’s engagement.

Focus on benefits, not features - emphasize how your product/service is of value

When selling any product or service focus on the benefits rather than the features. Prospects are looking for solutions to their problems. By putting the features of your product or service in the context of how it will benefit them, you can make a powerful and memorable impression.

Think about the reason why your customer will be interested in your product or service. What are the tangible or intangible benefits can they expect? Does it enhance their efficiency, ease collaboration, or save on time and expenses? Understanding why they should come to you is crucial in crafting a compelling sales email.

Include relevant Call-to-Actions for High ROI Email

Call to action is an indispensable tool for sales professionals. A successful sales email should contain relevant and persuasive call-to-action statements that let the prospect know what to do next. Including relevant call-to-actions in sales emails is important for two main reasons.

  1. It increases the likelihood that the email will be converted as it encourages prospects to take action rather than merely reading and forgetting about the email.
  2. It establishes a trusting relationship between the salesperson and the customer. By including a call-to-action in the email, the customer is likely to view the salesperson as thoughtful and reliable, which may encourage them to continue the relationship and eventually make a purchase.

These call-to-action statements can be as simple as sending an email to book an appointment or signing up for an email list. They can also be more complex, urging the prospect to make a purchase or download a piece of content. The call-to-action should be tailored to the individual customer and their needs, so it should be relevant and timely.

Keep it short and sweet - write concise and attention-grabbing copy

You need to keep your sales emails short and sweet. Too long, and the recipient may not even bother to read it. Too short, and they may not understand the point you're trying to make or get the full message. Keep it short and to the point. Avoid jargon or overly elaborate language, and make sure the main points of your message are expressed clearly.

Your goal should be to draw the reader in and make them want to learn more about your product or service. Include only the necessary contact and company information, use formatting options such as bullet points to make your email more reader-friendly and of course, friendly and inviting language goes a long way in creating a positive customer experience. Keep emails concise, persuasive and easy to navigate, and you'll reap the benefits!

Incorporate elements of design - create visuals to draw in readers

The fundamental element of any good sales email is making it pop. To make sure your sales email stands out in the inbox, incorporate elements of design to help draw the reader’s attention.

  • Design elements such as solid colour blocks or subtle, attractive backgrounds can hint at what services you’re offering and make the email look more inviting, prompting the reader to learn more.
  • Use typography that’s consistent with your brand rather than copy-pasting a generic template. Remember to include images or GIFs, that convey an important point or supplement the content. This helps make your email more contextual, unique, and distinct.
  • Consider the use of video, which can be a strong tool to show the recipient what they could benefit from, rather than simply including text.

All these elements help create impactful sales emails that target copywriters, making sure they recognize the true potential of your offer.

What are the benefits of writing Sales emails with AI?

AI and automation can be used to create personalised messages that are tailored to the customer’s interests and meet their needs. Automation makes it easier to reach out to many people quickly, saving time and resources, as well as reducing human error. AI algorithms can track each customer’s engagement and make sure you’re sending the right message to the right person at the right time. The use of AI for email writing enables you to create well-crafted marketing emails that are specific to your customers’ needs. Here are the benefits of using AI for sales emails:

  1. Helps source and use relevant facts, figures, and data to craft personalised, engaging emails that are tailored to their readers.
  2. Can identify popular words and phrases to further enhance the effectiveness of sales emails.
  3. AI-driven automation enables you to craft compelling content quickly and efficiently without spending hours manually writing and editing every email.
  4. Reduces spam and improves the quality of email campaigns overall.

The takeaway? With the help of AI, you can create emails that engage readers and drive conversions – a key factor to success in any email marketing campaign.
